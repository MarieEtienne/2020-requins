Observation protocol

The observations were made from a plane at 200m altitude and going at an average speed of 180kmh, in the Bay of Biscay off La Rochelle in France. The method used to collect data is a transect sampling which consist of following lines (transects) and counting the occurences of sharks as well as their distances from the plane.

Statistical methods

Detection function

A detection function is used to define the probability of an individual to be observed and take account of observation bias.

R Function: Distance::ds
Covariates and key function selection: minimization of the Akaike criterion
Covariate used: Seastate (Beaufort scale)
Key function used: hazard-rate
Probability equation:
\[\Large 1-\frac{1}{e^{(\frac{\sigma}{d})^b}}\]

Density function

Density surface modelling of line transect data is a Generalized Additive Model-based approach. It is used to calculate spatially-explicit estimates of animal abundance with data from observations and covariates.

R Function: dsm::predict
Covariates selection: Forward selection with minimization of the Akaike criterion
Covariates used: Sea surface temperature, chlorophyll A and localization (X,Y coordinates, Lambert93).
Availability bias: potentially visible individuals* (Nykanen)
Three “availability” levels are taken into account: 1, 0.41 and depending on the location on the continental shelf (on-shelf/off-shelf).
Distribution: Negative binomiale/Tweedie distribution
