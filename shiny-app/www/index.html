Data

The observations were made from a plane at 200m altitude and going at an average speed of 180km / h, in the Bay of Biscay off La Rochelle. The method used to collect data is a transect sampling which consist of following lines (transects) and counting the occurences of sharks as well as their distances from the plane.

Sessions

Four sessions were carried out in 2019 :
→ Session 1 from 12/02 to 27/02
→ Session 2 from 30/05 to 02/06
→ Session 3 from 31/07 to 08/08
→ Session 4 from 25/10 to 19/11

Transect Sampling

Our transects

Detection function

A detection function is used to define the probability of an individual to be observed and take account of observation bias.
Function : Distance::ds
Covariates selection : minimum Akaike information criterion
Covariate used : Seastate (Beaufort scale)
Function used : hazard-rate

Density function

Density surface modelling of line transect data is a Generalized Additive Model-based approach is used to calculate spatially-explicit estimates of animal abundance from distance sampling (also presence/absence and strip transect) data.
Covariates selection : Forward selection with a minimum Akaike information criterion
Covariates used : Sea surface temperature, chlorophyll A and localization.
Availability : potentially visible individuals* (Nykanen)
Three “availability” levels are taken into account : 1, 0.41 and onshelf/offshelf
